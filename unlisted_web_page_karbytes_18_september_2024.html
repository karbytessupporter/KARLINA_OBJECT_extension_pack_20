Suppose I upgrade from a free WordPress.com account to a Personal WordPress.com account and create a website whose domain name ends with “wordpress.com” with that Personal WordPress.com account. Suppose I add approximately 5GB of data to that website. Then suppose, after a year, I stop paying for the Personal plan. Will my 5GB of data or website disappear?

ChatGPT:

If you upgrade from a free WordPress.com account to a Personal plan and add 5 GB of data, but later stop paying for the plan and revert back to the free plan, here’s what happens:

1. Storage Limit: The free plan has a storage limit of 1 GB. If your site contains 5 GB of data and you stop paying for the Personal plan (which offers 6 GB of storage), you will exceed the free plan’s storage limit.

2. Access to Data: Your content will not immediately disappear. However, WordPress.com will restrict your ability to add new content or files to the site until your total storage usage falls back under the free plan’s 1 GB limit.

3. Potential Restrictions: Your site will remain publicly accessible, but you may face restrictions or limitations, such as not being able to upload more files, add media, or even edit some content until you’re back within the free plan’s limits.

4. Domain and Features: If you were using any premium features (such as a custom domain), those will no longer be available, and your site would revert back to a domain ending in “wordpress.com.”

To avoid issues, you would need to either remove enough content to get under the 1 GB limit or resubscribe to a paid plan that accommodates your storage needs.
